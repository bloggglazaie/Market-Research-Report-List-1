<p><p>The hot-dip galvanized steel wire market comprises two primary types: pure zinc and zinc alloy. Pure zinc coating involves immersing steel wire in molten zinc, providing robust corrosion resistance suitable for various applications. In contrast, zinc alloy coatings incorporate additional metals, enhancing properties like adhesion and durability. Zinc alloy wires are often preferred in harsher environments due to their superior protective qualities. Both types serve industries such as construction, agriculture, and automotive, offering distinct advantages based on specific project requirements.</p></p>

<p><p>Inorganic corrosion inhibitors are chemical compounds that are utilized to prevent or minimize the corrosion of metals and alloys in various environments. These inhibitors function by forming a protective layer on the metal surface or by altering the electrochemical processes that lead to corrosion. Common types of inorganic corrosion inhibitors include phosphates, nitrites, and silicates, which are widely employed in industries such as oil and gas, water treatment, construction, and automotive.</p><p>The Inorganic Corrosion Inhibitors Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 6.9% during the forecast period.

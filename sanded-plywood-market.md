<p><p>The sanded plywood market is categorized into three classes based on quality and intended use. Class I Plywood is high-quality, featuring a smooth finish and is used in applications requiring aesthetic appeal, such as furniture and cabinetry. Class II Plywood has a moderate finish, suitable for construction projects where appearance is less critical but strength is prioritized. Class III Plywood exhibits a rougher finish and is typically used for structural applications, such as subfloors and sheathing, where visual factors are not essential.</p></p>

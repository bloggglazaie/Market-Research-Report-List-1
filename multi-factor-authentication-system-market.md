<p><p>Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) systems enhance security by requiring multiple forms of verification. Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) combines something you know (like a password) with something you have (like a smartphone). Three-Factor Authentication (3FA) adds a third layer, such as biometric data. Four-Factor Authentication (4FA) includes a fourth element, often contextual information like location. Five-Factor Authentication (5FA) further enhances security with multiple biometrics or behavioral analytics, creating a comprehensive security approach suitable for high-risk environments and sensitive data protection.</p></p>

<p><p>The influenza diagnostic market comprises various testing methodologies. Rapid flu detection tests provide quick results for influenza A and B, utilizing antigen detection methods. Virus detection involves isolating and identifying the virus from specimens, offering high specificity. Direct Fluorescent Antibody (DFA) tests use labeled antibodies to visualize viral antigens in samples, facilitating prompt diagnosis. Serological testing measures antibodies in the blood, helping determine past infections or immunity levels. Each type plays a crucial role in accurate and timely influenza diagnosis and management.</p></p>
